ChlorEco - Sustainable biotechnology for feed supplements
ChlorEco was developed at the Department of Chemical Engineering, University of Almeria (Spain), as part of the Erasmus BIP: Sustainable Biotechnology Entrepreneurship: Microalgae & Human Food program. The project was a collaborative effort involving team members Bianca, Filippo, Georgi, Riccardo, and Serena. ChlorEco introduces an innovative approach to waste management and feed supplementation. The core of the project involves reusing coffee production wastewater as a growth substrate for microalgae, specifically Chlorella sp. This process not only treats wastewater to produce clean water but also generates a nutrient-rich substrate that can be utilized to enrich animal feeds. The integration of Chlorella-based supplements into feed formulations enhances the nutritional profile of animal products, leading to improved quality. Moreover, this process significantly reduces the environmental impact of both coffee production and livestock farming by mitigating issues such as eutrophication and methane emissions. ChlorEco exemplifies a circular economy model, where waste is transformed into a valuable resource, contributing to sustainable agriculture and environmental preservation.
